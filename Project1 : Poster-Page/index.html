Suryanamaskar

12 yoga poses.

Steps to Follow:

  1. Pranamasana (Prayer pose) : Stand straight, hands in prayer at chest, breathe evenly.
  2. Hastauttanasana (Raised arms pose) : Inhale, stretch arms up and back, biceps near ears.
  3. Hastapadasana (Standing forward bend) : Exhale, bend forward from waist, hands beside feet.
  4. Ashwa Sanchalanasana (Equestrian pose) : Inhale, right leg back, knee down, look up.
  5. Dandasana (Stick pose) : Inhale, left leg back, body in straight line.
  6. Ashtanga Namaskara (Salute with eight parts or points) : Exhale, touch floor with chin, chest, hands, knees, feet.
  7. Bhujangasana (Cobra pose) : Inhale, slide forward, raise chest into cobra pose.
  8. Adho Mukha Svanasana (Downward facing dog pose) : Exhale, lift hips into inverted ‘V’ shape.
  9. Ashwa Sanchalanasana (Equestrian pose) :Inhale, right foot forward between hands, look up.
  10. Hastapadasana (Standing forward bend) : Exhale, left foot forward, bend down fully.
  11. Hastauttanasana (Raised arms pose) : Inhale, rise with arms up and slight backbend.
  12. Tadasana (Mountain Pose) : Exhale, straighten up, arms down, relax.

*Surya Namaskar can be practiced 3-5 times a day. It's important to listen to your body and not overexert yourself, and to focus on proper form and breathing, rather than just the number of repetitions
